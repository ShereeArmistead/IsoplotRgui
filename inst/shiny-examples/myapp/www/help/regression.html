Legend:

  1. slope = s ± x | y | z

    where:

    s: the slope of the best fit line.
    x: the estimated uncertainty (σ) of s.
    y: the 100(1-α)% confidence interval for s using the appropriate number of degrees of freedom.
    z: the approximate 100(1-α)% confidence interval for s with overdispersion, calculated as z = y√MSWD.
  2. intercept = i ± x | y | z

    where:

    i: the y-intercept obtained by linear regression.
    x: the estimated uncertainty (σ) of i.
    y: the 100(1-α)% confidence interval for i using the appropriate number of degrees of freedom.
    z: the approximate 100(1-α)% confidence interval for i with overdispersion, calculated as z = y√MSWD. (only reported if MSWD > 1)
  3. MSWD = x, p(χ2) = y

    where:

    x: the Mean Square of the Weighted Deviates (MSWD) for the linear fit. MSWD values >> 1 indicate that the data are overdispersed with respect to the stated analytical uncertainties.
    y: the Chi-squared p-value for the linear fit. p-values << 0.05 indicate that the data are overdispersed with respect to the stated analytical uncertainties.

References:

York, D., Evensen, N.M., Martinez, M.L. and De Basabe Delgado, J., 2004. Unified equations for the slope, intercept, and standard errors of the best straight line. American Journal of Physics, 72(3), pp.367-375.
