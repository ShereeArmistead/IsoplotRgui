Legend:

  1. mean = t ± x | y

    where:

    t: the weighted mean age using a random effects model with two sources of uncertainty.
    x: the estimated uncertainty (σ) of t.
    y: the 100(1-α)% confidence interval for t using the appropriate number of degrees of freedom.
  2. MSWD = x, p(χ2) = y

    where:

    x: the Mean Square of the Weighted Deviates (MSWD) for age homogeneity. MSWD values >> 1 indicate that the ages are overdispersed with respect to the stated analytical uncertainties.
    y: the Chi-squared p-value for the age homogeneity test. p-values << 0.05 indicate that the ages are overdispersed with respect to the stated analytical uncertainties.
  3. dispersion = x | y

    where:

    x: the standard deviation of the true ages after removal of the analytical uncertainties. The dispersion is significantly greater than zero if the MSWD is significantly greater than one.
    y: the 100(1-α)% confidence interval for x.
