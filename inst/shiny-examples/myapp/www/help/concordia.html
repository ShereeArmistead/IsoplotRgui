The concordia diagram is a graphical means of assessing the internal consistency of U-Pb data. It sets out the measured 206Pb/238U- and 207Pb/235-ratios against each other ('Wetherill' diagram) or, equivalently, the 207Pb/206Pb- and 207Pb/235-ratios ('Tera-Wasserburg' diagram). The space of concordant isotopic compositions are marked by a curve, the 'concordia line'. Isotopic ratio measurements are shown as error ellipses. Concordant samples plot near to, or overlap with, the concordia line. They represent the pinnacle of geochronological robustness. Samples that plot away from the concordia line but are aligned along a linear trend form an isochron (or 'discordia' line) that can be used to infer the composition of the non-radiogenic ('common') lead or to constrain the timing of prior lead loss.

Legend:

  1. concordia age = t ± x | y | z

    where:

    t: the Maximum Likelihood estimate of the age using the algorithm of Ludwig (1998).
    x: the analytical uncertainty (σ) of t.
    y: the 100(1-α)% confidence interval for t using the appropriate number of degrees of freedom.
    z: the approximate 100(1-α)% confidence interval for t with overdispersion, calculated as z = y√MSWD.
  2. MSWD = u | v | w, p(χ2) = x | y | z

    where:

    u: the Mean Square of the Weighted Deviates (MSWD) for concordance.
    v: the MSWD for equivalence.
    w: the MSWD for concordance + equivalence.
    x: the Chi-squared p-value for concordance.
    y: the Chi-squared p-value for equivalence.
    z: the Chi-squared p-value for concordance + equivalence.

Legend:

  1. lower intercept = t ± x | y | z (Wetherill)
    upper intercept = t ± x | y | z (Wetherill)
    age = t ± x | y | z (Tera-Wasserburg)
    (207Pb/206Pb)o = t ± x | y | z (Tera-Wasserburg)

    where:

    t: the Maximum Likelihood estimate of the age or initial 207Pb/206Pb-ratio using the algorithm of Ludwig (1998).
    x: the analytical uncertainty (σ) of t.
    y: the 100(1-α)% confidence interval for t using the appropriate number of degrees of freedom.
    z: the approximate 100(1-α)% confidence interval for t with overdispersion, calculated as z = y√MSWD. (only reported if MSWD > 1)
  2. MSWD = u, p(χ2) = v

    where:

    u: the Mean Square of the Weighted Deviates (MSWD) for the isochron fit.
    v: the Chi-squared p-value for the isochron fit.

References:
